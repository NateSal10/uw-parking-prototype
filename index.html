Help & FAQs

Everything you need to know

Understanding the Map
What do the colors mean?
Green: Plenty of spots available (20+ spaces)
Yellow: Filling up fast (5-19 spaces)
Red: Full or nearly full (0-4 spaces)
Gray: Closed for events or maintenance
What's the difference between Central and Peripheral lots?
Central Lots: Located near core academic buildings, shorter walk times, fill up quickly (E1, C1, N1, S1)

Peripheral Lots: Located on campus edges, often have more availability, shuttle service available (W18, E5)
How often is the data updated?
Parking availability is updated every 2 minutes using sensors at lot entrances. Look for the "Last Updated" timestamp on the map screen.
About Permits & Parking
What is a Student E Permit?
Student E permits allow parking in E-designated and W-designated lots. Valid Monday-Friday, 7 AM - 5 PM. Can be purchased quarterly from UW Transportation Services.
What does ADA mean?
ADA stands for Americans with Disabilities Act. ADA spots are accessible parking spaces reserved for people with disability permits. They're wider and located close to entrances.
What is EV Charging?
EV (Electric Vehicle) charging stations allow you to charge your electric or hybrid car while parked. Some lots have Level 2 chargers available on a first-come, first-served basis.
Using the App
How do I find parking for my class?
Tap "Find Parking" on the home screen, enter your building name (e.g., Kane Hall), and we'll recommend the best available lots based on walking distance and your permit type.
What if my usual lot is full?
The app will suggest alternative nearby lots automatically. You can also check the map to see all available options with color-coded availability.
Can I get alerts about my favorite lot?
Yes! Go to Profile ‚Üí Notification Settings and enable "High demand warnings" and "Spot availability updates" to get notified about your preferred lots.
